About

Background

I'm Alyssa Eilermann, a 24-year-old with a journey that's been as diverse as it's been rewarding. Born and raised in Saint Louis, Missouri, I started life as an only child with my mom by my side. After high school, I embarked on a quest for knowledge at the University of Missouri, only to realize after a year that my true path lay elsewhere. I took the leap into the world of full-time work, seeking my passion and life's calling. This quest led me to Kansas City, where I embraced new experiences for several years. However, my journey eventually led me back home to Saint Louis, where I cherished moments with my beloved brothers for a few months. Currently, I reside in Columbia, Missouri, with my boyfriend, supporting him as he furthers his education. Life's journey may have its twists and turns, but I'm excited to embrace each step as it comes

Web Development Journey

My journey into the world of web development had an unexpected and life-changing beginning. In the summer of 2022, while traveling through Europe, I crossed paths with a fellow traveler who exuded boundless enthusiasm while sharing her experiences as a web developer. Her joy and excitement ignited a spark within me, leaving me eager to explore this intriguing field. At that time, I found myself in a comfortable but unfulfilled phase of life, sensing that there was something more waiting for me. However, upon returning home, the demands of reality took precedence, and my newfound inspiration took a backseat. It wasn't until about six months ago that I recalled that transformative conversation and decided to take the leap. I delved into web development, starting with self-teaching through YouTube videos, reading documentation, and completing courses like FreeCodeCamp. In under a month, I dedicated over 300 hours to learning and earned a certification. My days were filled with coffee shop and library sessions, completely immersed in this journey. I woke up each day with excitement, eager to delve deeper into this world of coding and problem-solving. Building webpages from scratch, implementing my own ideas, and deciphering code became my passion. This journey not only revealed my aptitude for logic but also ignited a newfound love for the process of creation, embracing the blend of creativity and logic that web development offers.

Job History

My journey in the world of work began during my senior year of high school when I served as a camp counselor at a summer camp for children with autism. It was an incredibly fun and rewarding first job that left a lasting impression. Upon entering college and subsequently leaving after a year, I transitioned into a role as a pre-K assistant teacher at a daycare center, where I cherished the opportunity to witness the daily growth and learning of young minds. Relocating to Kansas City prompted a change, leading me to work as a front desk associate at an eyecare center, honing my organizational and customer service skills. Unfortunately, the outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic led to a layoff, and I took on various roles, from warehouse work to employment at Walmart. As the world began to stabilize post-pandemic, I resumed my job search, ultimately becoming a primary assistant and manager at Preferred Family Healthcare, where I contributed to the well-being of 10 clients with disabilities. My responsibilities included overseeing staff, handling administrative tasks, such as paperwork and appointments, and ensuring the smooth operation of the facility. My journey then led me back to Saint Louis, where I explored photography and took up Uber as a source of income. I continue with photography and Uber while pursuing my dream job as a web developer.

Challenges

Throughout my journey, I've encountered several challenges that have tested my resolve and determination. The feeling of not doing enough and the perception of slow progress often crept in, especially as I took on the role of both student and teacher. Self-guided learning meant creating my schedule and navigating my path, often unsure if I was on the right track. Imposter syndrome and doubts about my employability without a college degree occasionally cast shadows. Designing sleek, professional, and appealing web interfaces posed its own set of hurdles. Despite these challenges, each day presented new opportunities for growth and learning. I've come to realize that coding isn't about perfection but about continuous skill development. Keeping this mindset at the forefront has made the journey more manageable. I'm aware that everyone starts somewhere, and the only true failure is giving up. It's the persistence and the journey of learning and improvement that make this experience incredibly rewarding.

I aspire to work with a team of individuals who not only have a genuine enthusiasm for the field but are also eager to mentor and nurture my growth. Although my current focus is on web development, I remain open to future opportunities for growth and advancement within the industry.
